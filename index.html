Bill Gates

Business Magnate, Investor, and Philanthropist

Time line of Bill Gates's life

  • October 28, 1955: Bill Gates is born in Seattle, Washington, United States.
  • 1968:Gates attends Lakeside School, where he gains early exposure to computers and programming.
  • 1973: Gates enrolls at Harvard University but drops out after two years to pursue a career in software development.
  • 1975: Gates co-founds Microsoft with Paul Allen. They develop and sell BASIC interpreters for various computer platforms.
  • 1980: Microsoft partners with IBM to provide the operating system (MS-DOS) for IBM's personal computers, leading to widespread adoption.
  • 1985: Microsoft releases Windows 1.0, the first graphical user interface-based operating system.
  • 1990: Windows 3.0 is released, becoming a major success and establishing Microsoft as a dominant player in the software industry.
  • 1995: Windows 95 is launched, featuring significant improvements in user interface and functionality.
  • 2000: Bill Gates steps down as CEO of Microsoft, assuming the role of Chief Software Architect, focusing on long-term product strategy.
  • 2006: Gates announces his plan to transition away from full-time work at Microsoft and dedicate more time to philanthropy.
  • 2008: Bill and Melinda Gates establish the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, focusing on global health, education, and poverty alleviation.
  • 2014: Satya Nadella becomes the CEO of Microsoft, succeeding Gates.
  • 2018: Gates steps down from his role as Chairman of Microsoft's board to focus on his philanthropic activities.
  • 2019: Bill and Melinda Gates announce their divorce, but they continue to work together on the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.
  • Present: Bill Gates remains actively involved in philanthropy, investing in innovative solutions for global challenges and promoting public health initiatives.
